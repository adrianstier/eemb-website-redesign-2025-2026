# EEMB UCSB Website Modernization Plan
## Comprehensive Strategy for a Modern, Engaging Department Website

**Date:** November 12, 2025  
**Department:** Ecology, Evolution & Marine Biology, UC Santa Barbara  
**Project Lead:** Chair's Website Committee

---

## Executive Summary

The EEMB department requires a complete website redesign to accurately reflect its world-class research, diverse community, and forward-thinking vision. The current site (eemb.ucsb.edu) is dated, difficult to navigate, and fails to showcase the department's exceptional achievements in coral reef ecology, marine biology, and evolutionary research.

**Primary Goals:**
1. Create a modern, visually compelling website that attracts top prospective graduate students and faculty
2. Effectively communicate research excellence to donors and community stakeholders
3. Improve content discoverability and user experience across all audiences
4. Implement sustainable content management systems for long-term maintenance
5. Integrate new features: updated DEI section, In Memoriam page, and Good News digest

**Target Audiences (Priority Order):**
- Prospective graduate students
- Prospective faculty candidates
- Donors and funding partners
- Community members and public outreach
- Current students, staff, and faculty

---

## Current Site Audit: Key Findings

### Strengths to Preserve
- Strong research identity in ecology, evolution, and marine biology
- Rich faculty expertise and lab diversity
- Unique location assets (oceanfront campus, field sites)
- Established graduate program reputation
- Separate specialized microsites (diversity.eemb.ucsb.edu, gsac.eemb.ucsb.edu, etc.)

### Critical Weaknesses
1. **Dated Visual Design**: Layout feels 2010s-era with insufficient whitespace and poor typography
2. **Poor Information Hierarchy**: Key achievements and research excellence buried in text
3. **Weak Visual Storytelling**: Limited use of compelling fieldwork imagery, coral reef photos, or research visuals
4. **Fragmented Navigation**: Unclear pathways for different user types
5. **No News/Achievements Section**: Department successes not prominently featured
6. **Outdated DEI Content**: Still references old Working Group System
7. **No Memorial Section**: Recently deceased faculty presence simply removed
8. **Limited Mobile Optimization**: Responsive design but not mobile-first
9. **Inconsistent Lab Website Integration**: Individual lab sites poorly linked/showcased
10. **Content Silos**: Graduate vs undergraduate content poorly differentiated

### Content Inventory Needs
- Complete scrape of all existing pages, links, and content
- Photo and media asset audit
- External link database (to other UCSB sites, research facilities)
- Personnel directory with complete metadata
- Course catalog integration points
- Research area taxonomy development

## Design Vision & Principles

### Visual Identity
**Core Concept:** "Ocean of Discovery" - Clean, modern design inspired by coastal California and cutting-edge marine research

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: UCSB Navy (#003660) and UCSB Gold (#FEBC11) as base
- Accent: Ocean blues (light #4A9EC6, deep #1B5E7E) for differentiation
- Neutral: Clean whites, light grays (#F5F5F5), charcoal text (#333333)
- Highlight: Vibrant coral/orange (#FF6B35) for CTAs and key elements

**Typography:**
- Headings: Modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter, Montserrat) - clean and confident
- Body: Highly readable sans (e.g., Source Sans Pro, Open Sans)
- Scale: Mobile-first responsive hierarchy (18px body minimum on mobile)
- Line height: 1.6-1.8 for optimal readability

### Design Principles

#### 1. Hero-Driven Storytelling
- Full-width hero banner with rotating high-quality imagery:
  - Coral reef fieldwork in Mo'orea
  - Lab research in action
  - Student-faculty collaboration
  - Coastal California ecosystems
  - Kelp forest diving
- Dynamic tagline: "Advancing ocean science from genes to ecosystems"
- Immediate CTAs: "Apply to Graduate Program" | "Explore Our Research" | "Support EEMB"

#### 2. Audience-Centric Modules
Homepage organized in visual "chunks" for different audiences:

**Module 1: Prospective Graduate Students**
- Image: Lab meeting or fieldwork
- Heading: "Join Our Research Community"
- Brief: Funded PhD programs in world-class labs
- CTA: "Explore Graduate Program" â†’

**Module 2: Research Excellence**
- Image: Coral reef or data visualization
- Heading: "Pioneering Marine & Evolutionary Biology"
- Brief: From SBC LTER to Mo'orea coral regeneration
- CTA: "Discover Our Impact" â†’

**Module 3: Faculty Opportunities**
- Image: Faculty in field or teaching
- Heading: "Build Your Career at UCSB"
- Brief: Collaborative environment, unique coastal access
- CTA: "View Open Positions" â†’

**Module 4: Community & Outreach**
- Image: Public event or diversity initiative
- Heading: "Science for Everyone"
- Brief: Public lectures, FUERTE program, K-12 partnerships
- CTA: "Get Involved" â†’

#### 3. Navigation Architecture
**Primary Navigation (Horizontal):**
```
About | People | Research | Programs | News & Events | Give
```

**Secondary Navigation (Dropdowns):**
- **About**: Our Mission | History | Facilities | Visiting EEMB | Contact
- **People**: Faculty | Postdocs | Graduate Students | Staff | Alumni | In Memoriam
- **Research**: Research Areas | Labs & Centers | Field Sites | Publications | Spotlights
- **Programs**: Graduate (MS/PhD) | Undergraduate Opportunities | FUERTE | REU Programs
- **News & Events**: Good News Digest | Seminars | Symposia | Calendar | Media Coverage
- **Give**: Support Research | Student Fellowships | Contact Development

**Tertiary Navigation (Footer):**
- Quick Links: DEI Committee | GSAC | Safety | Computing Support
- External: UCSB Biology | MSI | Natural Reserves | LTER Sites
- Resources: Course Schedules | Handbooks | Forms | Room Reservations

#### 4. Mobile-First Optimization
- Burger menu with full-page overlay navigation
- Touch-friendly button sizes (minimum 44x44px)
- Stacked layouts with generous padding
- Optimized images (WebP format, lazy loading)
- Swipeable galleries and carousels
- Fast load times (<3 seconds on mobile)

#### 5. Visual Hierarchy Best Practices
- **H1**: 48-64px on desktop, 32-40px mobile - used once per page
- **H2**: 36-42px desktop, 28-32px mobile - major sections
- **H3**: 24-30px desktop, 22-26px mobile - subsections
- Bold and color used strategically, not excessively
- Icon system for quick scanning (research areas, resources)
- Card-based layouts for faculty, news, events
- Strategic use of full-width vs contained sections

#### 6. Engaging Visual Content
**Photography Priority:**
- Original fieldwork photography from Mo'orea, SBC LTER sites
- Lab research in action (microscopy, genomics, tank experiments)
- Student-faculty interactions and mentorship
- Diversity and community events
- Campus coastal location shots
- Before limiting stock photography, prioritize authentic departmental imagery

**Image Specifications:**
- Hero images: 1920x800px minimum, optimized to <200KB
- Module images: 800x600px, <100KB
- Faculty headshots: 400x400px, consistent backgrounds
- Research spotlights: 1200x675px (16:9 ratio)

**Video Integration:**
- Research lab tours (30-60 second clips)
- Student testimonials
- Faculty research highlights
- Virtual field site tours

#### 7. Calls to Action & Conversion
**Primary CTAs (High Visibility):**
- "Apply to Graduate Program" (Apply by Dec 1)
- "Support EEMB Research"
- "Contact Faculty"
- "Schedule a Visit"

**Secondary CTAs:**
- "Learn More About [Topic]"
- "View Research Publications"
- "Explore Lab Websites"
- "Sign Up for Newsletter"

**Tertiary CTAs:**
- Social media follows
- Event registrations
- Resource downloads

#### 8. Brand Consistency
- Unified button styles (primary: filled gold, secondary: outlined navy)
- Consistent card shadows and borders
- Icon library (Font Awesome or custom set)
- Spacing system (8px base unit: 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64px)
- Animation standards (subtle, purposeful - 200-300ms transitions)

#### 9. Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA Standard)
- Color contrast ratios â‰¥4.5:1 for text
- Alt text for all images
- Semantic HTML5 structure
- Keyboard navigation support
- ARIA labels for screen readers
- Captions for video content
- Skip navigation links
- Focus indicators on interactive elements

#### 10. Performance Optimization
- Image lazy loading
- CSS/JS minification
- CDN delivery for assets
- Browser caching strategies
- Google PageSpeed score target: >85
- Core Web Vitals optimization

#### Writing Guidelines for Professional Transformation

**Title Format:**
- **DON'T:** "Check out some new publications: Halley Froehlich is lead author..."
- **DO:** "Froehlich Publishes Critical Review on Sustainable Aquaculture"

**Best Practices:**
- Active voice, present tense
- 8-12 words maximum
- Lead with action or achievement
- Proper title case
- No casual phrases ("Check out", "Congratulations on")

**Excerpt/Summary (150 characters for cards):**
- **DON'T:** "She and her colleagues respond to a recent special issue..."
- **DO:** "New paper in *Reviews in Aquaculture* refocuses conversation on current challenges in sustainable aquaculture."

**Full Article Body (300-500 words):**

**Structure:**
1. **Lead paragraph:** Who, what, when, where, why (inverted pyramid)
2. **Context paragraph:** Significance, background, broader impact
3. **Details paragraph:** Specifics, methodology, quotes if available
4. **Related information:** Links, faculty associations, research areas

**Tone Guidelines:**
- Professional but not stuffy
- Celebrate without excessive praise
- Let achievements speak for themselves
- Include relevant context (e.g., "Professor X's third NSF grant")
- Avoid first-person pronouns ("we", "our")
- Focus on impact and significance

## Technical Requirements & Platform

### Content Management System Options

**Recommended: WordPress with Custom Theme**
- **Pros**: Most flexible, huge plugin ecosystem, easy for non-technical admins, good for blog functionality
- **Cons**: Requires security maintenance, can be overkill for simple needs
- **Best for**: If you want robust blog features for Good News, easy admin control

**Alternative: Drupal**
- **Pros**: More structured, better for complex data relationships, enterprise-grade
- **Cons**: Steeper learning curve, fewer themes, more developer-dependent
- **Best for**: If you have dedicated technical staff and need complex content relationships

**Alternative: Static Site Generator (Hugo, Jekyll) + CMS**
- **Pros**: Extremely fast, secure, version-controlled content
- **Cons**: Less user-friendly for non-technical editors, requires build process
- **Best for**: If you have developer resources and prioritize speed/security

### Hosting Requirements
- UCSB IT infrastructure (preferred for consistency)
- OR cloud hosting (AWS, Digital Ocean, Netlify)
- SSL certificate (HTTPS)
- CDN for media assets
- Regular automated backups
- Staging environment for testing

### Key Technical Features

**1. Responsive Framework**
- Bootstrap 5 or Tailwind CSS
- Mobile breakpoints: 320px, 768px, 1024px, 1440px
- Touch-friendly interface elements

**2. Search Functionality**
- Site-wide search (people, research areas, news)
- Autocomplete suggestions
- Filters (by person type, research area, date)
- Google Custom Search integration OR native search

**3. Database Integration**
- MySQL or PostgreSQL for relational data
- API endpoints for dynamic content (faculty directory, news feed)
- JSON feeds for labs, publications, events

**4. Forms**
- Contact forms (general, faculty-specific)
- Good News submission (Google Forms integration)
- Graduate application inquiries
- Event registration

**5. Analytics & Tracking**
- Google Analytics 4
- Heatmaps (Hotjar or similar) for UX insights
- Conversion tracking (application clicks, contact form submissions)
- Traffic source analysis

**6. Integrations**
- Google Calendar API (for events)
- Social media feeds (Twitter/X, Instagram, YouTube)
- UCSB course catalog
- Publication databases (Google Scholar, ORCID)
- External sites (diversity.eemb.ucsb.edu, gsac.eemb.ucsb.edu)

**7. Admin Interface**
- Role-based access control (admin, editor, contributor)
- Easy content editor (WYSIWYG)
- Media library management
- User management for personnel database
- Workflow approval (for sensitive content)

### Security & Compliance
- Regular security updates and patches
- HTTPS only (force SSL)
- UCSB IT security compliance
- GDPR/privacy policy (if collecting user data)
- Backup schedule (daily automated)
- Disaster recovery plan

## Risk Management

### Potential Risks & Mitigation

**Risk: Content Migration Errors**
- Mitigation: Thorough content audit and testing, maintain backup of old site, staged rollout
- Contingency: Keep old site accessible during transition period

**Risk: Technical Issues Post-Launch**
- Mitigation: Extensive QA testing, soft launch period, monitoring
- Contingency: Rapid response technical support, rollback plan

**Risk: Low Adoption/Engagement**
- Mitigation: User testing pre-launch, training sessions, clear documentation
- Contingency: Iterative improvements based on feedback

**Risk: Outdated Content**
- Mitigation: Clear governance structure, annual content audit schedule, automated reminders
- Contingency: Quarterly review by committee to catch gaps

**Risk: Security Vulnerabilities**
- Mitigation: Regular updates, UCSB IT compliance, security plugins, HTTPS only
- Contingency: Incident response plan, backup restoration procedure

**Risk: Budget Overruns**
- Mitigation: Detailed project scope, phased approach, prioritization of must-have vs nice-to-have
- Contingency: Scale back optional features, extend timeline

**Risk: Accessibility Compliance Issues**
- Mitigation: Design with accessibility from start, regular audits, WCAG 2.1 AA standard
- Contingency: Accessibility remediation as priority fix
